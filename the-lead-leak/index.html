Why this happens

It's not that you're bad at follow-up. You're just one person.

Every business owner we talk to already knows they should call back faster, quote faster, follow up sooner. That's not the problem. The problem is bandwidth. You're on the job, with a client, running the actual business, while the phone rings, the quote sits half-written, and the follow-up gets buried under the next thing. Falling behind on this stuff isn't a discipline problem. It's a math problem: one person can't be everywhere a lead needs them to be, all day, every day.
